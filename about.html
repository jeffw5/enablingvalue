The Work
Behind
the Work

My path into enterprise architecture began with a simple conviction: organizations fail not because they lack data, but because they lack meaning. After graduating with a BS in Mathematics from the University of Illinois in 1983, I spent the next four decades building the discipline that bridges those two realities — knowledge engineering.

At Verizon, I built the Knowledge Engineering practice from scratch — the first of its kind in the organization. Using GraphDB, OWL, RDF, SPARQL, SHACL, and SKOS, I constructed a Unified Semantic Knowledge Layer governing 30,000+ data assets across the enterprise. Not a project. A discipline.

Over 17 years spanning healthcare, pharmaceuticals, telecommunications, and aerospace, I've learned that the hardest problems in enterprise systems are never technical. They are ontological. What do we mean by this term? How does this concept relate to that one? What is the canonical source of truth — and who is accountable for it?

That conviction became the foundation of The Value Enablement Group — and of every framework I've developed since: the AI Circuit Breaker, RDSG v2.0, Holonic Semantic Architecture, and the Morphism-Grounded AI Assurance research targeting DARPA's DSO program.

Philosophy

The Values
Hierarchy

Not a slogan. A decision-making framework applied to every engagement, every architecture, every recommendation.

01
Planet
Every architectural decision must first ask: does this serve or harm the planetary systems that sustain all life? Technology that optimizes for extraction at the expense of regeneration is not architecture — it is a liability. I ground every engagement in Doughnut Economics and planetary boundary thinking.
02
People
Human dignity, agency, and flourishing come before economic efficiency. AI systems that cannot explain their decisions to the humans they affect are not trustworthy systems — they are power asymmetries encoded in software. My AI governance frameworks exist to restore that balance.
03
Profit
Economic sustainability is essential — but it is the outcome of right action, not its justification. Organizations that deliver genuine value to people within planetary boundaries will generate durable, compounding returns. That is what "enabling value" means.

Civic Initiative · Daily Practice
One Good Thing
A community initiative grounded in the conviction that institutional decay is fought one positive action at a time. One Good Thing is a daily practice of constructive engagement — finding, doing, and sharing one thing that builds rather than extracts, connects rather than divides, regenerates rather than depletes. It is the Planet > People > Profit values hierarchy in daily lived form.
